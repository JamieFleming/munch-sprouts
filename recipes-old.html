Smooth Purees for First Tasters (4-6 Months)

Avocado & Basil Puree

Easy

Creamy avocado-pea-basil puree perfect for 4-6 month beginners.

10 mins

12 portions

4-6 months

Ingredients

  • 200g frozen peas
  • ½ avocado (flesh only)
  • 10 fresh basil leaves
  • 50ml cooking water (reserved)/Baby's chosen milk

Instructions

  1. Boil peas for 5 minutes, drain (reserving 50ml cooking water).
  2. Rinse peas with cold water in sieve to cool quickly.
  3. Place cooled peas, avocado, basil leaves, and reserved water (or milk) in blender.
  4. Blend until smooth and creamy (add splash more water if needed).
  5. Serve immediately or store in airtight container in fridge (up to 48hrs).
  6. Frozen peas make this recipe super quick and baby-safe!

Tips & Notes

  • Frozen peas = no chopping, perfect for beginners!
  • Adjust water for desired consistency (thicker for spoon, thinner for bottle).
  • Great intro to green flavors - basil adds fresh twist!
  • Store 48hrs max in fridge - freeze in ice cube trays for portions.

Aubergine, Pepper & Sweet Potato Puree

Easy

Colourful roasted vegetable puree introducing aubergine and pepper flavours for 4-6 month olds.

25 mins

10 portions

4-6 months

Ingredients

  • Aubergine, cut into cubes
  • ½ small red pepper, chopped
  • Spray oil
  • 1 sweet potato (~250g), peeled & cubed
  • Baby's usual milk

Instructions

  1. Put aubergine and pepper in roasting tin or airfryer, add a spray of fry light.
  2. Cover with foil, roast at 200°C (180°C fan) for 15-20 mins OR 180°C for 10 minutes in the airfryer.
  3. Meanwhile boil sweet potato for about 15 minutes or until tender.
  4. Combine all veggies in a blender and blitz to puree or fine mash texture.
  5. Add baby's usual milk, blend until super smooth.

Tips & Notes

  • Foil keeps veggies moist and prevents drying out.
  • Adjust milk for perfect smooth consistency.
  • Great intro to roasted veggie flavours!
  • Freeze in ice cube trays for perfect portions.

Butternut Squash, Mint, Broccoli + Courgette Puree

Easy

Smooth vegetable puree suitable for early weaning from 4–6 months.

20 mins

10 portions

4–6 months

Ingredients

  • 1/2 butternut squash, peeled, deseeded + chopped
  • 5 fresh mint leaves
  • 1 courgette, diced
  • 1/2 small head broccoli

Method

  1. Place the squash in a saucepan, cover with water and bring to the boil, then reduce the heat, cover and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Add the broccoli + courgette and cook under a medium heat until soft then drain.
  3. Purée the vegetables in a food processor, or use a hand blender, until the mixture is smooth.
  4. For a super smooth puree, pass it through a sieve after blending if weaning early.

Tips & Notes

  • Allow to cool fully before serving.
  • Add a little cooled boiled water if a thinner consistency is preferred.
  • Can be frozen in small portions for later use.
  • Ensure completely smooth texture for early weaning.

Butternut Squash, Sweetcorn + Carrot Puree

Easy

Butternut squash, sweetcorn and carrot puree for 4-6 months.

25 mins

21 portions

4-6 months

Ingredients

  • ½ butternut squash (about 400 g), peeled + cubed
  • 2 Carrots
  • ½ Can no salt/sugar sweetcorn, drained

Method

  1. Steam or boil the butternut squash and carrots in a saucepan over a medium heat for 10 minutes until almost cooked.
  2. Add the sweetcorn + cook for 5 minutes more until everything is tender.
  3. In a food processor or using a hand blender, purée with 5 tablespoons boiled water (or baby's milk) until smooth.

Tips & Notes

  • Steam rather than boil to retain more nutrients.
  • Add boiled water gradually for the desired consistency.
  • Blend thoroughly until completely smooth for younger babies.
  • Freeze in ice cube trays for convenient portions.

Brussels Sprouts Puree

Easy

Brussels sprouts puree for 4-6 months.

20 mins

30 portions

4-6 months

Ingredients

  • 150g Brussels sprouts
  • Extra water or baby's milk

Method

  1. Cut off the base of the sprouts and remove the outer leaves.
  2. Cut the sprouts in halves or quarters and steam over a medium heat for about 10-12 minutes until very tender (steaming is best as boiled sprouts can taste bitter).
  3. Blend the sprouts with 4-5 tablespoons of boiled water in a food processor, or using a hand blender, until smooth.

Tips & Notes

  • Steaming reduces bitterness compared to boiling.
  • Remove outer leaves for a smoother texture.
  • Blend thoroughly to avoid fibrous texture.
  • Freeze in small portions for later use.

Carrot Puree

Easy

Carrot puree for 4-6 months.

15 mins

5 portions

4-6 months

Ingredients

  • 3 carrots (about 250 g), peeled and halved lengthways
  • Water/Baby's Milk

Method

  1. Slice the carrots into half-moon shapes, ~5 mm thick. Steam or boil the carrots in a saucepan over a medium heat for 10-12 minutes until completely tender.
  2. Blend the carrots with 3-4 tablespoons of boiled water (or baby's milk) in a food processor, or using a hand blender, until completely smooth.

Tips & Notes

  • Slice evenly to ensure consistent cooking.
  • Steam to retain nutrients.
  • Add water gradually for desired thickness.
  • Freeze in ice cube trays for easy portions.

Broccoli Puree Recipe

Easy

Broccoli puree for 4-6 months.

15 mins

3 portions

4-6 months

Ingredients

  • ½ small head broccoli (about 130 g), cut into small florets

Method

  1. Steam or boil the broccoli in a saucepan over a medium heat for 8 minutes until very tender.
  2. Add 2-3 tablespoons boiled water, blend the broccoli in a food processor until smooth.

Tips & Notes

  • Steam to prevent waterlogging.
  • Cut florets small for even cooking.
  • Blend until completely smooth.
  • Portion and freeze for convenience.

Bright Start Breakfast Bowl

Easy

Mashed texture breakfast bowl for babies learning to chew.

15 mins

2 portions

Ingredients

  • 1 banana, peeled, cut into thirds and frozen
  • 2 broccoli florets (about 40g/1½ oz)
  • 1 tablespoon rolled oats
  • 1½-2 tablespoons whole milk or milk of choice, plus extra if needed
  • 1 ripe baby avocado, halved, stoned and flesh scooped out
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • Lightly toasted bread, buttered and cut into fingers, to serve

Method

  1. Take the banana out of the freezer to soften slightly while you prepare the rest of the ingredients.
  2. Meanwhile, steam the broccoli in a saucepan over a medium heat for 8 minutes or until the florets are tender (you can cook them in a microwave or an airfryer). Place under cold running water until cold.
  3. Place the oats in a food processor or blender and blitz to a powder, then pour in the milk.
  4. Add the softened banana, avocado, cinnamon and cooked broccoli.
  5. Blend until smooth and creamy, adding a splash more milk if needed – it should have the consistency of a very thick milkshake.
  6. Spoon the mixture into bowls and serve with fingers of lightly toasted bread for dunking.

Tips & Notes

  • Adjust milk to achieve desired thickness.
  • Ensure broccoli is fully cooled before blending.
  • Great introduction to thicker textures.
  • Best served fresh.

Herby Scrambled Egg

Easy

Soft scrambled egg with herbs for babies learning to chew.

10 mins

1 portion

Ingredients

  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • 1 tablespoon whole milk or milk of choice
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 5g 1/8 oz unsalted butter
  • 10g 1/4 oz frozen chopped spinach, defrosted
  • Lightly toasted bread, buttered and cut into fingers, to serve

Method

  1. In a bowl, beat the egg with the milk and oregano.
  2. Melt the butter in a small saucepan over a medium-low heat. Add the egg mixture to the pan and cook, stirring continuously, for 1-2 minutes until scrambled.
  3. Stir in the spinach and warm through briefly.
  4. Serve the eggs with fingers of toasted bread on the side.

Tips & Notes

  • Cook gently for soft, fluffy texture.
  • Ensure spinach is fully defrosted and drained.
  • Cut toast into manageable finger pieces.
  • Serve warm, not hot.

My First Chicken Curry

Easy

Mild chicken curry with mashed texture for babies learning to chew.

30 mins

2 portions

Ingredients

  • 2 teaspoons olive oil
  • 1 skinless chicken breast (about 100g)
  • 30g / 1oz white basmati rice, rinsed
  • 30g / 1oz frozen peas
  • 30g / 1oz cauliflower florets, finely grated
  • 1/4 teaspoon mild curry powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 200ml / 7oz unsweetened coconut drinking milk or whole milk or milk of choice

Method

  1. Heat the oil in a small saucepan over a medium heat. Add the chicken and cook, turning once, for 4 minutes or until starting to turn golden. Remove from the pan and set aside.
  2. Add the rice to the pan with the peas, cauliflower and spices and mix until combined. Place the chicken breast on top of the rice mixture. Pour over 175ml of the coconut milk or milk and bring to the boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover with a lid and simmer for 12-15 minutes until the rice and chicken are cooked.
  3. Remove the chicken, roughly chop, then place in a mini food processor with the remaining coconut milk or milk and process until very finely chopped.
  4. Using a hand blender, mash the cooked rice and pea mixture in the pan to a coarse puree, adding more milk if needed and making sure the peas are broken down. Stir in the chopped chicken into the rice mixture and serve.

Tips & Notes

  • Ensure chicken is fully cooked before blending.
  • Add extra milk if mixture becomes too thick.
  • Mash well to reduce choking risk.
  • Suitable for freezing in small portions.

Pea and Ham Bake

Easy

Family pasta bake suitable from 7-9 months.

30 mins

4 portions

7-9 months

Ingredients

  • 100g white pasta
  • 2 mushrooms (35g)
  • 1/2 small onion, peeled (35g)
  • 1 garlic clove, peeled and crushed
  • 1/2 tbsp fresh basil
  • 40g ham
  • 1 tsp vegetable oil
  • 100g tinned chopped tomatoes (1/4 can)
  • 1 tsp tomato puree
  • 40g frozen peas
  • 50ml water

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C / 350°F / Gas Mark 4.
  2. Bring a pan of water to the boil, cook the pasta for 10 to 12 minutes until soft. When cooked, drain and place in an oven proof dish, making sure the pasta only half-fills the dish.
  3. Chop the mushrooms, onions, garlic, basil and ham.
  4. Heat the oil in a saucepan and add the onions, garlic and mushrooms until softened (2 to 3 minutes).
  5. Add the chopped tomatoes, tomato purée, water and peas – stir, cover and simmer for 5 to 10 minutes (until the sauce has thickened slightly).
  6. Add the basil and diced ham.
  7. Pour the sauce over the pasta and mix well. Place in the oven for 10 minutes.
  8. Allow to cool. Blend or mash the pasta bake until smooth with soft lumps. Serve a quarter in a bowl with fork or spoon.

Tips & Notes

  • Ensure pasta is very soft before blending.
  • Mash to a texture suitable for your baby’s stage.
  • Can be enjoyed by the whole family.
  • Cool fully before serving.

Creamy Hotpot

Easy

Family favourite hotpot suitable for all ages.

40 mins

4 portions

Ingredients

  • ½ medium leek
  • ½ medium carrot, peeled
  • 1 small potato, peeled
  • 1 tbsp green beans
  • 1 small chicken breast fillet
  • ½ tbsp vegetable oil
  • 150ml water
  • 1 small spear broccoli
  • 1 tbsp full-fat cream cheese

Method

  1. Chop the leeks, carrots and potatoes. Trim and chop the beans. Dice the chicken.
  2. Heat the oil in a large pan. Add the chicken and fry for 2 to 3 minutes.
  3. Now add the leeks, carrots, potatoes, green beans and water, bring to the boil, cover and simmer for about 20 minutes until the chicken and vegetables are cooked. If you'd like extra sauce, add a little more water during cooking.
  4. Meanwhile, bring a small pan of water to the boil, add the broccoli and cook for 3 to 4 minutes until soft (or microwave if you prefer).
  5. Turn the hotpot off the heat and stir in the cream cheese.
  6. Blend or mash the cooked hotpot until it is smooth with soft lumps. Serve ¼ of the hotpot with the cooked broccoli as a finger food.

Tips & Notes

  • You could replace the chicken for haddock if you fancy trying a fish dish.
  • Add extra water if more sauce is needed.
  • Mash according to your baby’s chewing stage.
  • Allow to cool before serving.

Cheese and Mushroom Cakes

Easy

Tasty cheese and vegetable cakes introducing a combination of flavours.

40 mins

10 portions (approx)

6 months +

Ingredients

  • 1 medium potato (150g), peeled
  • Half a small leek (60g)
  • 3 mushrooms (50g)
  • 1 tbsp fresh chives (optional)
  • 75g full-fat hard cheese
  • 1 medium egg
  • 1 tsp vegetable oil
  • 2 heaped tbsp plain flour
  • 50g breadcrumbs

Method

  1. Dice the potatoes then boil until tender (around 10 minutes). Drain and mash.
  2. Finely slice the leeks and dice the mushrooms. Roughly chop the chives (if using). Grate the cheese. Beat the egg.
  3. Heat the oil in a pan, add the leeks and mushrooms. Cook until softened (4–5 minutes).
  4. Add the cheese, leeks, mushrooms and chives to the mash potato. Mix well. Pre-heat oven to 180C (350F, gas mark 4).
  5. Shape into 8–10 small patties that fit in the palm of your hand.
  6. Put the flour, beaten egg and breadcrumbs into 3 separate bowls. Coat each cake in turn (flour first, then egg and lastly breadcrumbs).
  7. Place on a baking tray and bake for 15–20 minutes, until golden brown.
  8. Once cooled, serve 1 cake for your baby to pick up and eat.

Tips & Notes

  • Easy for on the go.
  • Ensure cakes are fully cooled before serving.
  • Can be frozen and reheated thoroughly.
  • Always check texture is soft enough for your baby's age.

Simple Baked Fish

Easy

A simple way to introduce fish from 6 months.

25 mins

4 portions (approx)

6 months +

Ingredients

  • 1 salmon fillet or other chosen fish

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180C (fan) or airfryer.
  2. Place the salmon in an ovenproof dish, cover, and bake for about 20 minutes.
  3. Allow to cool and check carefully for any bones.
  4. Flake a quarter of the salmon into a bowl and serve.

Tips & Notes

  • Aim for 2 portions of fish a week.
  • Include 1 oily fish such as salmon.
  • Always double-check for bones before serving.
  • Allow to cool fully before giving to baby.

Crustless Mini Quiche

Easy

Soft mini quiches perfect for baby-led weaning.

25 mins

10 portions

6 months +

Ingredients

  • 1–2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 5 spring onions (optional)
  • Half medium pepper (any colour), deseeded
  • 1 small tomato
  • 5 medium eggs
  • 2 tbsp full-fat milk
  • 1 tsp dried mixed herbs (optional)

Method

  1. Pre-heat the oven or airfryer to 200C. Lightly grease a muffin tray.
  2. Finely dice the spring onions (if using), peppers and tomatoes.
  3. Distribute the vegetables between the muffin tray holes.
  4. Beat the eggs, milk and herbs together and pour over the vegetables.
  5. Bake for 10–15 minutes, until puffy, light brown and the eggs are set.
  6. Cool for a few minutes before serving.

Tips & Notes

  • Allow to cool fully before serving.
  • Ensure egg is completely cooked through.
  • Can be stored in fridge for up to 2 days.
  • Great for batch cooking.

Steamed Apple and Pear

Easy

Soft fruit fingers perfect for little hands.

15 mins

4 portions (approx)

6 months +

Ingredients

  • 1/2 medium apple, peeled and cored
  • 1/2 small pear, peeled

Method

  1. Chop into finger-sized wedges big enough for baby to grasp.
  2. Bring water to the boil and steam the pear fingers for 8 minutes.
  3. Add the apple fingers and cook for a further 2 minutes until softened (not collapsed).
  4. Allow to cool before serving.

Tips & Notes

  • Ensure fruit is soft enough to mash between fingers.
  • Allow to cool fully before serving.

Yummy Fish Pie

Easy

Comforting fish pie suitable from 6 months when blended smooth.

30 mins

2 portions

Ingredients

  • 2 x medium potatoes
  • 150g sweetcorn
  • 120ml water
  • 1 frozen fish portion of your choice (110g)
  • Sprinkle of parsley
  • 1 tbsp cream cheese

Method

  1. Chop and boil potatoes until soft.
  2. Steam the fish until cooked through and flake, checking carefully for bones.
  3. Cook the sweetcorn until hot, add 120ml water and blend until perfectly smooth.
  4. Add the sweetcorn puree to the potato and mash until smooth.
  5. Stir in the flaked fish and chopped herbs. Add cream cheese to help consistency.
  6. Blitz until smooth for 6 months.

Tips & Notes

  • Always check thoroughly for bones.
  • Blend completely smooth for younger babies.
  • Allow to cool before serving.
  • Adjust texture based on baby's age.

Cheesy Tomato Pitta Pizzas

Easy

Simple cheesy pitta pizzas suitable from 10 months.

10 mins

2 portions

10 months +

Ingredients

  • 1 wholemeal pitta bread
  • 2 tbsp tomato puree
  • 15g cheese (or dairy free alternative), grated

Method

  1. Spread the tomato puree on the pitta bread.
  2. Sprinkle with cheese.
  3. Grill or airfry until cheese has melted but the pitta is still soft.
  4. Cut into finger sized pieces.

Baked Bean Breakfast Pancakes

Easy

Quick and easy breakfast pancakes.

4 mins

8 portions

Ingredients

  • 1 x medium egg
  • 50g reduced sugar & salt baked beans
  • 20g self-raising flour
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika
  • Fresh chopped chives
  • Spray oil

Method

  1. Place the flour, egg + paprika together in a bowl and whisk.
  2. Add the beans + chopped chives and stir them in.
  3. Put some frylight in a pan over a low heat.
  4. Put 1 tbsp of your pancake mix in a pan at a time.
  5. Cook for ~2 minutes on one side only.
  6. Add tasty toppings like steamed fruit or avocado for the perfect breakfast pancakes!
  7. Refrigerate cooked pancake and use within 2 days.

Tips & Notes

  • Cook on low heat to avoid burning.
  • Serve warm, not hot.
  • Store in fridge for up to 2 days.
  • Great for breakfast or snacks.

Smoky Bean One-Pot Hotpot

2 portions

Ingredients

  • ½ tin reduced salt baked beans
  • 1 small onion
  • 1 cooked potato
  • 2 tablespoons sweetcorn

2 portions

Cheesy Broccoli + Potato Gratin

Easy

Perfect for exploring new textures.

45 mins

4 portions

Ingredients

  • 120g broccoli florets
  • 450g thinly sliced potato
  • 100ml milk
  • 70ml single cream
  • 50g grated cheese

Method

  1. Slice potato lengthways into thin strips.
  2. Gently heat the milk and cream and add the grated cheese. Heat until all the cheese has melted.
  3. Layer a thin layer of potato in the bottom of a dish.
  4. Add the broccoli evenly across the potato. Top with the remaining potato.
  5. Pour the cheese sauce over the top, ensuring to wet the whole layer (the sauce won't cover the top).
  6. Bake in the oven at 190°C for 35 minutes.
  7. Mash down finely for little ones from 7 months or more chunky for older babies.

Tips & Notes

  • Remember you can cook with cows milk but babies shouldn't have this to drink until 12 months.
  • Slice potatoes thinly for even cooking.
  • Allow to cool before serving.
  • Adjust texture depending on age.

On the Go Porridge Bars

Easy

Breakfast top up or an on the go snack.

30 mins

6 portions

Ingredients

  • 40g porridge oats
  • 175ml whole milk or milk of choice
  • 15g raisins or other dried fruit, very finely chopped
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 10g unsalted butter

Method

  1. Line a small baking tray with baking parchment. Place the oats + milk in a small saucepan + bring to the boil, then reduce the heat + simmer for 8 minutes, stirring frequently, or until the oats are very soft + mushy – it should be a thick porridge consistency.
  2. Transfer to a bowl + stir in the raisins and cinnamon, then spread out the porridge on the prepared baking sheet in an even layer about 1/2in thick. Cover and chill for ~30 minutes or until set.
  3. Cut the porridge into finger sized pieces.
  4. Melt the butter in a large, non-stick frying pan and cook the fingers for 3 minutes on each side or until golden. Leave to cool slightly before serving.

Tips & Notes

  • You can add any fresh/dried fruit depending on your baby’s taste.
  • Ensure dried fruit is finely chopped.
  • Cool before serving.
  • Store in fridge if not eaten immediately.

Fruity Delicious Porridge

Easy

Perfect for a warm breakfast.

10 mins

1 portion

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp (20g) porridge oats
  • 120ml full-fat milk or Dairy Alternative
  • 1/4 medium banana
  • 10g frozen berries if your choice
  • 1tsp plain full-fat yoghurt or Dairy Alternative

Method

  1. Put the porridge oats and milk into a saucepan. Heat up, stirring constantly, until the porridge thickens.
  2. Reduce the heat and simmer gently for 2 to 3 minutes, stirring until the oats are cooked.
  3. Mash the banana and stir the blueberries into the porridge and heat through until completely soft.
  4. Add a little more milk if your baby prefers it a little runnier.
  5. Pour the porridge into a bowl, add the yogurt and serve with the banana sticks.

Tips & Notes

  • If making this for children over 5 years of age, full-fat should be switched to a lower fat option.
  • Ensure berries are fully softened.
  • Serve warm, not hot.
  • Adjust consistency with extra milk if needed.
