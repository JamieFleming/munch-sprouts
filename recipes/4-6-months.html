Smooth Purees for First Tasters (4-6 Months)

Avocado & Basil Puree

Easy

Creamy avocado-pea-basil puree perfect for 4-6 month beginners.

10 mins

12 portions

4-6 months

Ingredients

  • 200g frozen peas
  • ½ avocado (flesh only)
  • 10 fresh basil leaves
  • 50ml cooking water (reserved)/Baby's chosen milk

Instructions

  1. Boil peas for 5 minutes, drain (reserving 50ml cooking water).
  2. Rinse peas with cold water in sieve to cool quickly.
  3. Place cooled peas, avocado, basil leaves, and reserved water (or milk) in blender.
  4. Blend until smooth and creamy (add splash more water if needed).
  5. Serve immediately or store in airtight container in fridge (up to 48hrs).
  6. Frozen peas make this recipe super quick and baby-safe!

Tips & Notes

  • Frozen peas = no chopping, perfect for beginners!
  • Adjust water for desired consistency (thicker for spoon, thinner for bottle).
  • Great intro to green flavors - basil adds fresh twist!
  • Store 48hrs max in fridge - freeze in ice cube trays for portions.

Aubergine, Pepper & Sweet Potato Puree

Easy

Colourful roasted vegetable puree introducing aubergine and pepper flavours for 4-6 month olds.

25 mins

10 portions

4-6 months

Ingredients

  • Aubergine, cut into cubes
  • ½ small red pepper, chopped
  • Spray oil
  • 1 sweet potato (~250g), peeled & cubed
  • Baby's usual milk

Instructions

  1. Put aubergine and pepper in roasting tin or airfryer, add a spray of fry light.
  2. Cover with foil, roast at 200°C (180°C fan) for 15-20 mins OR 180°C for 10 minutes in the airfryer.
  3. Meanwhile boil sweet potato for about 15 minutes or until tender.
  4. Combine all veggies in a blender and blitz to puree or fine mash texture.
  5. Add baby's usual milk, blend until super smooth.

Tips & Notes

  • Foil keeps veggies moist and prevents drying out.
  • Adjust milk for perfect smooth consistency.
  • Great intro to roasted veggie flavours!
  • Freeze in ice cube trays for perfect portions.

Butternut Squash, Mint, Broccoli + Courgette Puree

Easy

Smooth vegetable puree suitable for early weaning from 4–6 months.

20 mins

10 portions

4–6 months

Ingredients

  • 1/2 butternut squash, peeled, deseeded + chopped
  • 5 fresh mint leaves
  • 1 courgette, diced
  • 1/2 small head broccoli

Method

  1. Place the squash in a saucepan, cover with water and bring to the boil, then reduce the heat, cover and simmer for 10 minutes.
  2. Add the broccoli + courgette and cook under a medium heat until soft then drain.
  3. Purée the vegetables in a food processor, or use a hand blender, until the mixture is smooth.
  4. For a super smooth puree, pass it through a sieve after blending if weaning early.

Tips & Notes

  • Allow to cool fully before serving.
  • Add a little cooled boiled water if a thinner consistency is preferred.
  • Can be frozen in small portions for later use.
  • Ensure completely smooth texture for early weaning.

Butternut Squash, Sweetcorn + Carrot Puree

Easy

Butternut squash, sweetcorn and carrot puree for 4-6 months.

25 mins

21 portions

4-6 months

Ingredients

  • ½ butternut squash (about 400 g), peeled + cubed
  • 2 Carrots
  • ½ Can no salt/sugar sweetcorn, drained

Method

  1. Steam or boil the butternut squash and carrots in a saucepan over a medium heat for 10 minutes until almost cooked.
  2. Add the sweetcorn + cook for 5 minutes more until everything is tender.
  3. In a food processor or using a hand blender, purée with 5 tablespoons boiled water (or baby's milk) until smooth.

Tips & Notes

  • Steam rather than boil to retain more nutrients.
  • Add boiled water gradually for the desired consistency.
  • Blend thoroughly until completely smooth for younger babies.
  • Freeze in ice cube trays for convenient portions.

Brussels Sprouts Puree

Easy

Brussels sprouts puree for 4-6 months.

20 mins

30 portions

4-6 months

Ingredients

  • 150g Brussels sprouts
  • Extra water or baby's milk

Method

  1. Cut off the base of the sprouts and remove the outer leaves.
  2. Cut the sprouts in halves or quarters and steam over a medium heat for about 10-12 minutes until very tender (steaming is best as boiled sprouts can taste bitter).
  3. Blend the sprouts with 4-5 tablespoons of boiled water in a food processor, or using a hand blender, until smooth.

Tips & Notes

  • Steaming reduces bitterness compared to boiling.
  • Remove outer leaves for a smoother texture.
  • Blend thoroughly to avoid fibrous texture.
  • Freeze in small portions for later use.

Carrot Puree

Easy

Carrot puree for 4-6 months.

15 mins

5 portions

4-6 months

Ingredients

  • 3 carrots (about 250 g), peeled and halved lengthways
  • Water/Baby's Milk

Method

  1. Slice the carrots into half-moon shapes, ~5 mm thick. Steam or boil the carrots in a saucepan over a medium heat for 10-12 minutes until completely tender.
  2. Blend the carrots with 3-4 tablespoons of boiled water (or baby's milk) in a food processor, or using a hand blender, until completely smooth.

Tips & Notes

  • Slice evenly to ensure consistent cooking.
  • Steam to retain nutrients.
  • Add water gradually for desired thickness.
  • Freeze in ice cube trays for easy portions.

Broccoli Puree Recipe

Easy

Broccoli puree for 4-6 months.

15 mins

3 portions

4-6 months

Ingredients

  • ½ small head broccoli (about 130 g), cut into small florets

Method

  1. Steam or boil the broccoli in a saucepan over a medium heat for 8 minutes until very tender.
  2. Add 2-3 tablespoons boiled water, blend the broccoli in a food processor until smooth.

Tips & Notes

  • Steam to prevent waterlogging.
  • Cut florets small for even cooking.
  • Blend until completely smooth.
  • Portion and freeze for convenience.
