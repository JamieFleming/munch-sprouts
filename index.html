What is Baby Led Weaning?

Baby Led Weaning (BLW) is an approach to introducing solid foods where babies feed themselves from the very start, rather than being spoon-fed purees. Babies explore food at their own pace — building independence, developing motor skills, and learning to enjoy a variety of flavours and textures.

Why Choose BLW?

Research-backed benefits of baby led weaning for your little one.

Develops Independence

Babies learn to self-feed and make choices about what they eat, laying the foundation for a healthy relationship with food from the very start.

Improves Motor Skills

Picking up food develops hand-eye coordination and fine motor skills — including the pincer grasp — through natural, enjoyable practice.

Easier Family Mealtimes

Baby joins the family table and eats what everyone else eats. No more preparing separate purees — just adapt portions and textures.

Reduces Picky Eating

Early exposure to a wide variety of flavours, textures, and colours helps build a more adventurous palate and can reduce fussy eating later on.

How to Get Started

Three simple steps to begin your baby's weaning journey.

1

Check for Readiness Signs

Look for the three key signs: sitting upright with support, losing the tongue-thrust reflex, and showing interest in food. Most babies are ready at around 6 months — never before 17 weeks.

Learn about readiness signs →
2

Learn the Basics

Understand safe food preparation, which foods to avoid, how to introduce allergens, and the difference between gagging and choking. Knowledge is confidence.

First foods guide → Safety guide →
3

Start Simple & Track Progress

Begin with soft, easy-to-grasp single-ingredient foods. Log what your baby tries, track allergen introductions, and watch their skills and confidence grow.
